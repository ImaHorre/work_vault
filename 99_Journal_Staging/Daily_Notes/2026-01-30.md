## General
- Hot emboss troubleshooting and device testing

## Testing
- Reinforcement attempt: connector bond at 95°C for longer time
- Result: Narrow V5 channels/needle holes collapsed significantly, causing high flow resistance
- Hot emboss in lab assessment: not suitable for thermoplastic embossing (insufficient pressure/air pockets)
- Device testing results:
  - 10um UV device: BO at start and ends at normal operating conditions
  - 1um: no flow through diagonal
  - 30um UV: BO/monodispersity issues similar to 10um V5 UV

## Observations
- V5UV analysis: BO size/location measured through various parameters
- No significant change in BO location/size between 5ml/hr vs 1ml/hr at 100-600mbar
- Some signs of BO size increase at same locations
- Hot emboss equipment limitations identified
- Channel collapse issue with high temperature bonding

## Follow-up
- **Key learnings documented**:
  - S100 creates hydrophilic coating on blank COC (dilutions to 30%)
  - UV replica process working but needs refinement
  - V5 designs show oil wetting issues (possibly ECOC lid related)
  - Connector clamping needed for 1um high-pressure applications

### Strategy

- Standardised cell lines first, patient-derived samples later. 
- The platform needs to be reliable and reproducible before you add the complexity and variability of patient material. The pharma use case is high-throughput drug screening, so uniform droplet size and cell number per droplet are the priorities.

### Technical workflow

- Getting droplets into 96-well plates (or other standard formats) is something we need to think on.
- Oana mentioned cell pre-mixing before droplet formation and how to maintain cell ratios during mixing, to match that of the systems they are meant to represent. Overall throughout the project the system should be able to handle single-cell droplets through to large-scale multi-cell configurations.

### Cell types and models
 
- Moving from intestinal to pancreatic cells. 
- Tumour models are complicated, high cell numbers, heterogeneous populations, so ratio control and validation will matter a lot. 
- Start with standardised cell lines then adding patient-derived data (cell proportions, molecular subtypes) is a later stage once we've shown the platform works.

### Scaling

- PE has inherent capability of large numbers of droplets $\therefore$ many cell cultures. 
- Patient derived models don't necessarily fit that scale 
	- why? sample size too small? not required level of depth for further screening?
  so cell lines are the right tool for demonstrating reproducibility and throughput to industries like pharma/drug development. This is recommended as our first beachhead. 

### Work packages

- **WP3** is the initial testing phase, organ-on-chip-style, scalable, standardised lines. 
- **WP4** moves to more complex systems for demonstration, not necessarily limited to patient-derived models.

### Hydrogel / matrix

- Matrigel is not needed. Aman/Oana's lab runs on culture media and incubation, and that should be fine. Technical sharing meeting will be set up /task set up discussion with KTH/PE for initial generation of w/o using similar cell culture medium. 
- Whether any hydrogel is required at all is still not entirely known. 
- Other system types, other hydrogels, may have to later be considered. 

### Other 

- Simple systems first, then work toward complex co-culture setups. 
Droplet size and single vs. co-culture conditions are the key variables. Keeping parameters flexible for now.

- Single design, single cell line to start. Patient samples introduce too much variability too early. The target is an in vitro model that credibly approximates in vivo conditions.

- PE will use early project to explore limit of drop size capability.

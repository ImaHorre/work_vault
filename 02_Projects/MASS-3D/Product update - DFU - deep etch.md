Previously we had only etched up to 10um (for 30um diameter droplets). For the w/o droplets required for the cell cultures to grow it is supposed that much larger diameters are required. 

Larger droplets mean that the smallest dimension of the DFU needs to be larger than what we have made previously. 

Issues:
- Can the deep-etch resist be applied in a thicker layer? 
	Currently, we etch the DFU features first, add a layer of resist and then etch the deeper channels. The resist we add in the second step is 10um thick and needs to be thick in order to handle both the topography of the DFUs, and the etching processing.  What would happen if the primary etch was 50um deep? 
- Deeper main channels?
	How deep can we realistically go across all stages of the fabrication (master, shim, replica)? I would imagine the replica step is the limitation (and really only for R2R - limited by overall film thickness)
